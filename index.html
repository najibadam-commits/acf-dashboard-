WASH Compliance Radar
Priority WASH Gaps
⚠ Handwashing stations (19%): Only 57 of 306 sites had functional handwashing with soap. Immediate NFI replenishment and infrastructure repair required.
⚠ Latrine hygiene (39% clean): Most latrines observed as dirty or with overflow; 46% lack privacy locks and 64% are not sex-segregated. Risk of disease transmission and GBV exposure.
⚑ Daily water access (68%): 99 of 306 respondents reported water not available daily. Solar pump functionality checks recommended.
✓ Water quality perceived positively (75%) and no diarrhea reported by 77% of households in the last three months.
Water vs. Sanitation Performance
🔴 Critical Findings (Immediate Action Required)
⚠WASH — Handwashing facilities: Only 19% of WASH sites (57/306) had functional handwashing with soap. Immediate restocking and rehabilitation needed.
⚠WASH — Latrine condition: Only 39% of latrines observed clean; 46% lack privacy locks and 64% are not sex-segregated. Female beneficiaries at heightened GBV risk.
⚠Food — Harassment / exploitation: 33% of food beneficiaries (30/90) reported harassment or requests for favors at distribution sites. Root-cause investigation and staff re-training required.
⚠Health — Electricity: Only 48% of health facilities (105/217) had reliable electricity. Cold chain integrity and maternal delivery safety at risk. Solar backup procurement critical.
🟡 Moderate Concerns (Monitor Closely)
⚑Food — Weighing scale accuracy: Only 40% of vendor scales (36/90) were accurate. Recalibration / replacement needed to protect entitlements.
⚑WASH — Daily water supply: 32% of water points not available daily (99/306). Suspected pump failures or power outages. Asset maintenance checks needed.
⚑WASH — Hygiene kit coverage: Only 25% of WASH respondents (77/306) confirmed receiving a hygiene kit. Verify distribution lists and gaps.
⚑Protection — Discrimination: 5% of respondents (52/1,038) reported abuse or discrimination — down sharply from earlier rounds but still requiring do-no-harm follow-up.
🟢 Positive Findings (Maintain & Document)
✓Complaint awareness up to 85%: 883 of 1,038 beneficiaries now know at least one way to raise a complaint — a major AAP improvement across the period.
✓Zero unofficial fees: No beneficiaries reported paying unofficial fees for any service — strong accountability record.
✓Overall dignity 98%: 1,018 of 1,038 respondents believe ACF services protect their rights and dignity as beneficiaries.
✓Safety near-universal: 97% felt safe accessing services and 99% were treated with respect and dignity by staff.
✓OTP quality strong: 99% of OTP clients confirmed RUTF receipt and screening equipment/registers were consistently in place.
✓MHPSS satisfaction 99%: Across 123 sessions, empathy, privacy and clear explanation of purpose were near-universal.
