WASH Compliance Radar
Priority WASH Gaps
⚠ Handwashing stations (14%): Only 3 of 22 sites had functional handwashing with soap. Immediate NFI replenishment and infrastructure repair required.
⚠ Latrine hygiene (32%): 15 latrines observed as dirty or with overflow. Risk of disease transmission and GBV exposure (no privacy locks).
⚠ Daily water access (50%): 8 communities reported water not available daily. Solar pump functionality checks recommended.
✓ Water quality perceived positively (91%). No diarrhea reported. Hygiene promotion 100% coverage confirmed.
Water vs. Sanitation Performance
🔴 Critical Findings (Immediate Action Required)
⚠WASH — Handwashing facilities: Only 14% of WASH sites had functional handwashing with soap. Immediate restocking and rehabilitation needed across Gajiganna cluster.
⚠WASH — Latrine condition: 68% of latrines dirty/overflowing, no privacy locks. Female beneficiaries at heightened GBV risk. Emergency latrine rehabilitation needed.
⚠Protection — Discrimination: 20% of respondents reported experiencing discrimination based on displacement status. Staff re-orientation on do-no-harm principles required.
⚠Health — Electricity: 57% of health facilities without reliable electricity. Cold chain integrity and maternal delivery safety at risk. Solar backup procurement critical.
🟡 Moderate Concerns (Monitor Closely)
⚑AAP — Complaint awareness: 36% of beneficiaries unaware of any complaint channel. CFM sensitization campaign recommended at next outreach cycle.
⚑WASH — Daily water supply: 50% of water points not available daily. Suspected pump failures or power outages. Asset maintenance checks needed.
⚑Nutrition — RUTF coverage: 31% of OTP clients did not confirm RUTF receipt. Stock-out or distribution process gap to be investigated at facility level.
⚑Safety — 20% felt unsafe: 7 respondents did not feel safe; 2 were unsure. Root cause analysis (insecurity vs. facility conditions) needed at site level.
🟢 Positive Findings (Maintain & Document)
✓IPC Compliance 100%: All health staff observed washing hands, using sterile instruments, and properly disposing waste.
✓Zero unofficial fees: No beneficiaries reported paying unofficial fees for any service — strong accountability record.
✓Overall dignity 100%: Every single respondent (45/45) believes ACF services protect their rights and dignity as a beneficiary.
✓Hygiene promotion 100%: All WASH beneficiaries received hygiene messages and hygiene kits — behaviour change programming is functional.
✓No diarrhea reported: Zero diarrhea cases in the last 3 months across all WASH communities surveyed — positive health outcome.
✓Nutrition registers 100%: OTP, immunization tally sheets, and monthly summary reports all updated at visited sites.
